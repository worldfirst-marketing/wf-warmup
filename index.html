02 · WHY THIS IDEA
02
The problem, and how this fixes it

Our Laver Cup deal has no people in it.

Problem

No faces to build on

Our rights: a logo, a booth, some tickets. No player images. No Alcaraz in the UK. Campaigns need people — we have none.

Fix

Hire our own star

We pay one well-known player directly. Their face, name and footage become ours to use — fully cleared, for a year.

Fix

Hold it the week before

Not during the tournament — one week earlier. No event restrictions apply, and London's tennis buzz amplifies us for free.

One rule: we never use the words "Laver Cup" in the creative. The timing, the city and the red-blue look make the connection for us — legally and cleanly.

03 · THE EVENT
03
One court · one afternoon · about 3 hours

One point against a pro.

How it runs
  • 1 — The arrival. The player walks on. Short welcome, a bit of showmanship.
  • 2 — The gauntlet. Everyone gets one point against the pro. About 90 seconds per person, so 60+ people can play in an hour.
  • 3 — The team match. Guests split into a Blue team and a Red team for a short doubles tie. The pro plays a set for each side. Trophy for the winners.
  • 4 — The prize. Win your point → £100 of FX credit (needs compliance sign-off). Everyone gets their own video clip by QR code within minutes.
Why it works

One point is winnable enough that everyone tries. Every point is a ready-made 20-second video with a beginning, middle and end. Clients attend by private invitation, which keeps us inside the hospitality rules.

11 · RULES & NEXT STEPS
11
Stay clean · move fast

The rules, and what happens next.

Rules we follow

  • Never say "Laver Cup" in our creative. The player is "a British tennis legend". Timing does the rest.
  • Check the player has no deal with another payments or banking brand before we sign.
  • £100 FX credit is a financial promotion → compliance sign-off before launch.
  • Clients attend by private invitation only — no public ticket giveaways.
  • Everyone on camera signs a filming release. Usage rights written into the player's contract.
  • Show the plan to the sports-sponsorship team before the agency brief goes out.

The agency brief — ready to send

"UK fintech seeks a well-known British tennis name (retired preferred) for one London appearance day around 16 September: playing one-point challenges against staff and guests, light filming, 12 months' use on our own channels. No conflicts with payments/banking sponsors. Budget £10–45k depending on profile. Please send options, fees and availability by 25 July."
