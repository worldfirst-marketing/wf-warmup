PART 1:
THE
WARM-UP.

We hire a famous tennis player. We put them on a London court one week before the Laver Cup. Our clients, partners and employees play three short games against them. We film everything.

The hero prop in every shot: the physical WorldFirst World Card, in the star's hand. Part 2 of this deck covers everything we run during Laver Cup week itself — and the combined goals.

02 · WHY THIS IDEA
02
The problem, and how this fixes it

Our Laver Cup deal has no people in it.

Problem

No faces to build on

Our rights: a logo, a booth, some tickets. No player images. No Alcaraz in the UK. Campaigns need people — we have none.

Fix

Hire our own star

We pay one well-known player directly. Their face, name and footage become ours to use — fully cleared, for a year.

Fix

Hold it the week before

Not during the tournament — one week earlier. No event restrictions apply, and London's tennis buzz amplifies us for free.

The theme

The World Card is the hero prop

Every key image puts the physical WorldFirst World Card in the star's hand — held up like a trophy, signed like an autograph card, laid on the net like a challenge. One product, one image system, across the whole campaign.

One rule: we never use the words "Laver Cup" in the creative. The timing, the city and the red-blue look make the connection for us — legally and cleanly.

03 · THE EVENT
03
One court · one afternoon · about 3 hours

One point against a pro.

How it runs
  • The arrival. The star walks on and holds up the World Card: "Win a game, and I'll sign it."
  • Game 1 · The One-Point Showdown. You — or you and a teammate — against the pro. One point. Ninety seconds of glory, or a great story either way.
  • Game 2 · Return the Rocket. The pro serves at full speed. Touch it and the crowd roars. Return it and you go into the grand draw twice.
  • Game 3 · The Derby — Antom (Blue) vs WorldFirst (Red). Sister brands, one trophy. Guests drafted into two squads; the star plays a set for each side.
  • The grand draw. Game winners go into the hat. On court at the end: Laver Cup hospitality tickets for the weekend + the signed WorldFirst gift set. Drawn inside our private event, which keeps it within the ticket rules.
Why it works

One point is winnable enough that everyone tries. Every point is a ready-made 20-second video, and every player gets their clip by QR within minutes. Clients attend by private invitation, which keeps us inside the hospitality rules.

Guest list: 50 clients & partners · 20 KOLs & ambassadors · plus our employees. Everyone plays.

The gift shelf — WorldFirst-branded, signed live by the star
  • The Big Card — a giant World Card replica, signed and framed. The grand-draw hero gift.
  • Red WorldFirst rackets and ball tubes, signed on the day
  • Your own fight-poster print, signed by the star

Nothing Laver-Cup-branded — we don't hold merch rights, and we don't need them.

07·B · AMPLIFICATION
7B
Voices that carry this beyond our own channels

Three megaphones on the court.

The star's voice

The player amplifies us

A Henman-tier name brings national recognition and broadcast relationships. We write one post on the player's own channels into the contract, and we invite their media contacts to the court.

KOLs on court

Creators play the gauntlet

We invite UK business and sport creators to take their one point — losing to a legend IS the content. They post their own clips to their own audiences. We also buy them O2 tickets for tennis-week follow-up content.

Brand ambassadors

Clients with a voice

Our brand-ambassador clients get court time, a photo with the star, and first priority in the grand draw. PR builds a story around the flagship ambassador — their networks are exactly our buyer audience.

Twenty of the seventy guest places are reserved for KOLs and brand ambassadors. Every guest leaves holding ready-made content: their clip, their photo with the star, their intro card. Amplification is built into the event, not bolted on afterwards.

11 · RULES & NEXT STEPS
11
Stay clean · move fast

The rules, and what happens next.

Rules we follow

  • Never say "Laver Cup" in our creative. The player is "a British tennis legend". Timing does the rest.
  • Check the player has no deal with another payments or banking brand before we sign.
  • Clients attend by private invitation only. Laver Cup tickets may be prizes inside the closed event — never in public marketing.
  • All gifts are WorldFirst-branded, signed live by our hired star. We produce nothing with Laver Cup branding.
  • Everyone on camera signs a filming release. Usage rights written into the player's contract.
  • Show the plan to the sports-sponsorship team before the agency brief goes out.

The agency brief — ready to send

"UK fintech seeks a well-known British tennis name (retired preferred) for one London appearance day around 16 September: playing short challenge games against staff and guests, signing a set of WorldFirst-branded gifts, light filming, 12 months' use on our own channels. No conflicts with payments/banking sponsors. Budget £10–45k depending on profile. Please send options, fees and availability by 25 July."

Decisions needed this week

  • Approve concept + budget band (Lean / Standard / Flagship)
  • Approve the gift set (the Big Card, rackets, posters)
  • Confirm the date window (~Sept 16)
  • Name the owner for the client invite list
