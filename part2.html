14 · INSIDE VS OUTSIDE
14
Why our money goes outside the gates

Inside is blue. Outside is ours.

The reality

Every camera angle inside the arena carries Alipay+ — that's the contract, and it reaches 234 broadcast markets. In 2025 the in-arena quiz screens each session were A+-branded too. WorldFirst doesn't appear inside. So we spend where we're free:

  • Red OOH beside the A+ blue banner in London — our logo + "The Road to WorldFirst" (or sharper)
  • Employees in red in the fan zone — our own people, freely filmable
  • Online reuse — the banner creative runs again on our website and socials

Social rule stays: no player images — our people, our booth, our banners, the Laver logo only.

20 · ACT 2 GOALS
20
The rule from the call: every proposal carries one clear goal

Pick the goal first. Then the idea.

Agreed on the call: the purpose is not to show we sponsor the Laver Cup — it's to use the Laver Cup to drive one of three goals. Targets get numbers once quotas are confirmed.

Goal option 1

Brand awareness

  • Carried by: OOH red-beside-blue, online reuse, fan-zone reds
  • Measure: reach and UK brand lift during the week
Goal option 2

Customer engagement

  • Carried by: breakfast seats, VIP club places, booth visits, signed gifts
  • Measure: the right ~10 people hosted well (6 breakfast + 4–6 VIP, TBC)
Goal option 3

Customer acquisition

  • Carried by: KOL content, ticket competition, One Point Slam clips shared on LinkedIn
  • Measure: leads and new accounts from the campaign window

Per Sylvia's point: these are layers that run at different times, not competing options — but each proposal we submit names one primary goal.

21 · PROCESS & COMBINED VIEW
21
The agreed way forward, and both acts together

Three proposals. One combined report.

The process agreed on the call

  • 1 · Draft up to three proposals, each with one named goal (awareness / engagement / acquisition)
  • 2 · Share in the team group chat first — everyone adds ideas
  • 3 · Pick one or two options
  • 4 · Check with the sponsorship team (Daniel): is it doable, can any benefit be extended
  • 5 · Then take it to the UK team to execute
  • Julia leads the KOL component throughout

Both acts, one scorecard

  • Awareness: Act 1 film + clips, plus Act 2 OOH and online reuse — one combined reach number
  • Engagement: Act 1's 70 guests, plus Act 2's ~10 VIP seats (TBC) — every hosted name followed up
  • Acquisition: leads and new accounts across the whole campaign window — reported the way the team now measures: new-customer revenue as the headline, NFM as the secondary metric

One report after the tournament covers both acts — results first, reach second.
